Quick facts about the Argo program.

An Argo float is a robotic, autonomous instrument that drifts with ocean currents. It moves vertically through the water column, collecting data on temperature and salinity from the surface to 2,000 meters deep.
Scientists use Argo data to understand ocean circulation, heat storage, and the ocean's role in climate change. It is critical for improving climate models, long-range weather forecasts, and hurricane prediction.
