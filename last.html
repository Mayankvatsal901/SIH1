How an Argo Float Works

A 10-day journey of an autonomous profiling float, from deployment to data transmission.

1

Deployment & Drift

Floats are deployed and descend to a "parking" depth (around 1000m), where they drift with the currents for about nine days.

2

Data Collection

The float then descends to 2000m and begins its primary mission: a slow 6-hour ascent to the surface, measuring temperature and salinity.

3

Data Transmission

At the surface, the float transmits its collected data to a satellite. It then receives any new mission instructions and begins the 10-day cycle again.

Fascinating Ocean Facts

Dive into the mysteries of the deep with these incredible facts about our planet's oceans.

Why Don't the Atlantic & Pacific Oceans Mix?

It's a viral myth! They do mix. However, a visible line often appears due to differences in water density, temperature, and salinity. Fresher meltwater takes time to blend with saltier ocean water, creating a temporary, stunning visual boundary.

The World's Largest Waterfall is Underwater

The Denmark Strait Cataract, between Greenland and Iceland, is the mightiest waterfall on Earth, plunging over 11,500 feet (3,500 meters) and carrying 175 million cubic feet of water per second—dwarfing any land-based waterfall.

Earth's Longest Mountain Range is in the Ocean

The Mid-Ocean Ridge wraps around the globe for 40,389 miles (65,000 km). This massive feature is where new oceanic crust is formed through volcanic activity, playing a crucial role in plate tectonics.

The Argo Program: A 21st Century Revolution

The global Argo array was launched in 2000, deploying its first robotic floats. This marked a new era in oceanography, providing continuous, real-time data on a scale never before possible and revolutionizing our understanding of ocean climate.

Indian Ocean's Record-Breaking Heat

Argo floats have been crucial in detecting alarming warming trends. In recent years, the Indian Ocean has experienced unprecedented marine heatwaves, with surface temperatures soaring to record highs, impacting ecosystems and weather patterns.
