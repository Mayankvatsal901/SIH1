How Argo Floats Work

Precision-engineered autonomous profiling systems that execute systematic ocean measurements and satellite data transmission protocols.

Deployment & Positioning

Profiling floats are strategically deployed from research vessels and maintain operational positioning at designated parking depths of 1000-1500 meters during 10-day drift cycles.

Profiling & Data Collection

Following programmed cycles, floats execute controlled descents to 2000-meter depths. They then perform precision ascent profiles, creating detailed temperature and salinity profiles while continuously measuring pressure.

Data Transmission & Control

Upon surfacing, floats transmit complex data profiles, often packaged in scientific formats like NetCDF, via satellite. They also receive new mission parameters before starting the next 10-day cycle.

Fascinating Ocean Facts

Why Don't the Atlantic & Pacific Oceans Mix?

It's a viral myth! They do mix. However, a visible line often appears where they meet due to differences in water density, temperature, and salinity. The fresher, lighter meltwater from glaciers in the Gulf of Alaska takes time to blend with the saltier, denser water of the North Pacific, creating a temporary, stunning visual boundary.

The World's Largest Waterfall is Underwater

Forget Angel Falls. The Denmark Strait Cataract, located between Greenland and Iceland, is the mightiest waterfall on Earth. Cold, dense water from the Nordic Seas plunges over 11,500 feet (3,500 meters) beneath the warmer, lighter water of the Irminger Sea, carrying over 175 million cubic feet of water per secondâ€”dwarfing any land-based waterfall.

Earth's Longest Mountain Range is in the Ocean

The Mid-Ocean Ridge is a continuous underwater mountain chain that wraps around the globe for 40,389 miles (65,000 km), like the seam on a baseball. This massive geological feature is where new oceanic crust is formed through volcanic activity, playing a crucial role in plate tectonics and shaping the ocean floor.
